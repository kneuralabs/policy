KNEURALABS Policies
KNL-000 · Last reviewed April 2026

Who we are: KNEURALABS helps businesses use artificial intelligence (AI) safely and responsibly. Think of us as a trusted guide that makes sure AI is used in a way that is fair, legal, and good for everyone.

Who must follow these rules: Everyone — full-time employees, part-time workers, contractors, freelancers, and any outside partners we work with. If you do work for KNEURALABS in any way, these rules apply to you.

The golden rule: If you are ever unsure whether something is allowed, stop and ask before doing it. It is always better to ask than to guess.

Who is in charge?

  • Piyal Gupta – CEO: The top decision-maker. Has final say on all major matters.
  • Piyali Dhar – Managing Director: Oversees day-to-day operations and policy enforcement.

What happens if you break the rules?

  • A warning or formal disciplinary action.
  • Losing your job or contract with us.
  • In serious cases, legal action — for example if you steal data or commit fraud.
  • The consequences depend on how serious the violation is. We try to be fair.

How often do these rules change?

  • We review and update this handbook every year.
  • The next review is scheduled for April 1, 2026.
  • When rules change, we will tell you and ask you to acknowledge the new version.

Outside partners and vendors: If we hire an outside company or freelancer to help us, they must follow our rules too. We put this requirement in every contract we sign with them.

Speaking up (whistleblowing): If you see something that looks wrong — like someone breaking rules, cheating, or doing something illegal — please tell us. You will not get in trouble for reporting something in good faith, even if it turns out to be a misunderstanding. Silence can make things worse.

Do

  • Ask before doing — it's better than guessing.
  • Read every section before acknowledging.
  • Speak up if you see something wrong.
  • Follow these rules whether you're staff, contractor, or vendor.

Don't

  • Stay quiet about a rule violation — silence makes it worse.
  • Assume a rule doesn't apply to your role.
  • Skip the acknowledgment step.
  • Wait for the annual review to flag concerns.
