Workplace Conduct
KNL-ETH-001 · Last reviewed April 2026

This section is about how we treat each other, our clients, and the outside world. Good conduct is not just about following rules — it is about building a workplace where everyone feels respected and safe.

Honesty and integrity — the foundation of everything

  • Be truthful in everything you say and do — to colleagues, to clients, and to leadership.
  • Do not exaggerate your skills, experience, or results to impress clients or win projects.
  • If you make a mistake, own it and report it early. Covering up mistakes makes them worse.
  • Do not make promises to clients that you cannot keep, or that the company has not agreed to.

Conflicts of interest

  • A conflict of interest happens when your personal interests might influence your work decisions.
  • Examples:
    • You recommend a vendor who is a friend or family member without disclosing that relationship.
    • You own shares in a company that wants to become our client.
    • You are doing freelance work for a competitor on the side.
  • None of these automatically mean you did something wrong — but you must disclose them to your manager or the CEO before they affect your work.
  • When in doubt, disclose. It is always the right move.

Gifts and entertainment

  • You may accept small gifts (branded merchandise, food, etc.) worth up to $50 from clients or vendors without approval.
  • Gifts worth more than $50 must be declared to your manager and may need to be returned or donated.
  • Never accept gifts that feel like they are meant to influence a decision you are making — that is a bribe.
  • Meals or events paid for by clients are generally fine, but tell your manager if they feel lavish or unusual.

Equal opportunity — we treat everyone fairly

  • We hire, promote, and treat people based on their skills and performance — not based on who they are.
  • We do not discriminate based on: race, ethnicity, national origin, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, age, disability, religion, or any other protected characteristic.
  • If you need a reasonable accommodation (for example, a different keyboard due to a disability, or adjusted hours for a religious observance), ask HR. We will do our best to help.
  • Using slurs, stereotypes, or "jokes" based on these characteristics is not acceptable, even if "just kidding."

Anti-bribery and anti-corruption

  • Never offer or accept bribes — payments made to gain an unfair advantage.
  • Never pay "facilitation payments" — small bribes paid to speed up official processes (common in some countries, but illegal for US companies).
  • We follow the US Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA), which makes it illegal to bribe foreign government officials.
  • If someone asks you for a bribe or offers you one, refuse and report it to the CEO or legal team immediately.

Reporting misconduct — what to do if you see something wrong

  • Report any misconduct (rule-breaking, dishonesty, harassment, fraud) to our legal partner or directly to the CEO.
  • You can report anonymously if you prefer.
  • We take all reports seriously and investigate every one.
  • Retaliation is forbidden: If you report misconduct in good faith, no one is allowed to punish you, demote you, or make your job difficult because of it. If this happens, report it immediately.

Do

  • Be honest — own your mistakes early.
  • Disclose any conflict of interest to your manager.
  • Treat everyone with respect, no exceptions.
  • Report misconduct — anonymously if you prefer.
  • Decline gifts over $50 or check with your manager.

Don't

  • Inflate your skills or results to win business.
  • Offer or accept bribes or "facilitation payments."
  • Make jokes that target race, gender, religion, etc.
  • Cover up mistakes — they get worse with time.
  • Retaliate against someone who reported an issue.
These conduct standards apply to vendors and partners too. Everyone representing KNEURALABS — even indirectly — must uphold these values.
