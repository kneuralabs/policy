Policy Acknowledgment
KNL-ACK-001 · Last reviewed April 2026

You have reached the end of the KNEURALABS Policy Handbook. This final page explains what acknowledging the policies means, why it matters, and how to do it.

What does "acknowledging" mean?

  • By acknowledging, you are confirming that:
    • You have read all the policies in this handbook.
    • You understand what they require of you.
    • You agree to follow them in your day-to-day work.
  • This is not just a formality — it is a professional and in some cases legal commitment. Take it seriously.
  • If you have not finished reading all sections, please go back and do so before acknowledging.

What if I do not understand something?

  • That is completely normal — some policies cover complex topics.
  • Before acknowledging, ask your manager or the legal team to explain anything that is unclear.
  • Do not acknowledge policies you have not read or do not understand — it defeats the purpose.
  • There are no silly questions. We would rather you ask than guess.

How to send your acknowledgment

  • Click the "Acknowledge policies" link in the bottom left of the sidebar.
  • This will open your email app with a pre-filled message addressed to hello@kneuralabs.com.
  • Fill in your name and role, then send the email.
  • Keep a copy in your sent folder for your own records.

What happens after I acknowledge?

  • Your acknowledgment is recorded by the HR team.
  • You will be asked to acknowledge again each time the handbook is updated (annually, or sooner if major changes occur).
  • New team members must acknowledge within their first 5 working days.

Quick recap — the things that matter most

  • Never share passwords or client data with unauthorised people.
  • Only use approved AI tools. Never paste client data into public AI.
  • Get approval before spending company money.
  • Report security incidents, data breaches, and misconduct immediately.
  • Treat everyone with respect. Zero tolerance for harassment or bullying.
  • If in doubt, ask — never guess.
Thank you for taking the time to read this handbook. A team that understands and follows its policies is a team that clients can trust — and a team that is safer and better to work in for everyone.
