Finance & Expenses
KNL-FIN-001 · Last reviewed April 2026

This section explains how we handle money at KNEURALABS — approvals before spending, how to submit expense claims, how invoicing works, and what to do if you suspect financial wrongdoing.

Before you spend company money — get approval first

Every purchase needs approval. Here is a simple table:

  • Under $500: Ask your direct manager or the CEO/MD verbally or by email. Simple and quick.
  • $500 – $5,000: Ask the CEO or MD, and include a brief written note explaining what you need and why.
  • $5,000 – $25,000: Both the CEO and MD must agree in writing before you proceed.
  • Over $25,000: Both CEO and MD must agree and document the full business reason in writing. This takes more time, so plan ahead.
  • Never commit to a purchase and then ask for approval after. Get approval first, always.

Submitting expense claims — getting reimbursed for what you paid

  • If you paid for something out of your own pocket for work reasons, you can claim it back.
  • Submit your receipt within 15 days of the purchase. Late claims may not be paid.
  • Keep all receipts — digital photos are fine.
  • We do not reimburse: Personal items, family expenses, alcohol (unless at a company-approved event), or entertainment bills over $150 per person.
  • If you are unsure whether something is reimbursable, ask finance before you spend.

Invoicing — if you are a contractor or vendor

  • Send your invoice within 5 working days after completing the work or milestone.
  • All invoices must include your contract number or purchase order (PO) number. Invoices without these will be returned.
  • Payment is made within 30 days of receiving a correct invoice.
  • Send invoices to the finance contact listed in your contract.

Travel expenses

  • Book economy class for flights under 6 hours. Business class requires prior CEO/MD approval.
  • Hotels should be reasonably priced for the location — use your judgement. Luxury hotels require approval.
  • Use the company travel booking tool if one is provided. Ask your manager if unsure.

Fraud — cheating, stealing, or dishonesty with money

  • Fraud includes: submitting fake receipts, inflating expense claims, accepting kickbacks (secret payments from vendors), or using company money for personal purchases.
  • If you suspect fraud — even just a gut feeling — tell the CEO or MD immediately. You do not need proof to report a suspicion.
  • Whistleblowers are protected by law and by our policy. You will not be punished for reporting in good faith.
  • Anyone found committing fraud will face immediate termination and may face criminal charges.
For finance vendors and partners: Maintain complete audit logs, provide monthly financial reports by the 10th of each month, and store all financial records securely for at least 7 years as required by law.
