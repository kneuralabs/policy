Safe Workplace
KNL-SAFE-001 · Last reviewed April 2026

Everyone deserves to feel safe at work — physically, emotionally, and mentally. This section explains what behaviours are not tolerated and what you should do if you experience or witness something that makes you or someone else feel unsafe.

Sexual harassment — zero tolerance

  • Sexual harassment is any unwanted behaviour of a sexual nature that makes someone feel uncomfortable, humiliated, or threatened.
  • Examples of harassment include: unwanted touching or physical contact, sexual jokes or comments, sending inappropriate images or messages, repeatedly asking someone on dates after they have said no, and making comments about someone's body or appearance.
  • Harassment can happen in person, over text, email, video call, or on social media.
  • It does not matter whether the person "meant" to be offensive — if someone says it made them uncomfortable, it is taken seriously.
  • What to do: Report to HR, your manager, or the CEO. If you cannot report to your manager (because they are the problem), go directly to the CEO or our legal partner.
  • We will investigate within 15 working days and keep the report confidential where possible.
  • Anyone found to have committed sexual harassment will face disciplinary action up to and including termination.

Workplace violence and bullying

  • Workplace violence includes: physical threats, intimidation, bullying, aggressive behaviour, and any act that makes someone fear for their safety.
  • Bullying includes: repeatedly putting someone down, excluding someone from meetings or conversations on purpose, undermining someone's work, or making someone feel worthless.
  • Conflict and disagreement are normal — bullying is not. The difference is respect.
  • If you feel threatened or see someone being threatened, report it immediately. In an emergency, call 911 first.

Drugs and alcohol

  • No illegal drugs are permitted at any time — during work hours, at company events, or in any company space.
  • Alcohol is only permitted at company-approved events (for example, a team celebration). Even then, drink responsibly.
  • Never come to work impaired by alcohol, prescription drugs, or any other substance. This puts you and your colleagues at risk.
  • If you are struggling with substance abuse, please speak to HR privately. We will try to help.

Mental health and wellbeing

  • We recognise that mental health is as important as physical health.
  • If you are feeling overwhelmed, burnt out, or struggling, talk to your manager or HR. We will work with you, not against you.
  • Taking breaks, using your vacation days, and setting work-life boundaries is encouraged — not frowned upon.

Protection for people who speak up

  • If you report a safety concern, harassment, or any other issue in good faith, you are fully protected.
  • Retaliation — punishing someone for making a report — is itself a serious policy violation and may be illegal.
  • If you face retaliation after reporting something, report the retaliation immediately to the CEO or legal team.
These safety standards apply wherever KNEURALABS work is being done — in our office, at client sites, at remote locations, and at company events.
