Workplace & IT Rules
KNL-WP-001 · Last reviewed April 2026

This section covers the practical day-to-day rules for working at KNEURALABS — whether you work from the office, from home, or from a client site.

Remote work — working from home or another location

  • Use a secure internet connection: Your home Wi-Fi must be password protected. Public Wi-Fi in coffee shops, airports, or hotels is not secure enough for client work. If you must use public Wi-Fi, use the company VPN (ask IT for access).
  • Lock your screen: Every time you walk away from your computer — even for 30 seconds — lock it. Someone at home (a family member, flatmate, or visitor) should not be able to see client data on your screen.
  • Private workspace: Try to work in a private space when on calls that involve sensitive information. Avoid discussing client names or project details in shared spaces.
  • Same rules as the office: Working remotely does not mean different rules. Security, data handling, and conduct standards are identical whether you are in the office or at home.
  • If you are travelling internationally for work, tell IT beforehand — different countries have different security considerations.

Acceptable use of company devices

  • Company laptops, phones, and tablets are provided for work purposes. Limited personal use is fine (checking personal email, occasional browsing), but keep it minimal.
  • Forbidden uses: Running a personal business, illegal activities, downloading pirated software or media, storing large personal files, accessing inappropriate content, or hacking (even "ethical" hacking without IT approval).
  • No expectation of privacy: We may monitor activity on company devices. This is not about spying on you — it is to detect security threats. If you need to do something private, use your personal device.
  • Do not connect personal USB drives, hard drives, or unknown devices to company computers. These can carry viruses.

Physical security — protecting the office and its contents

  • ID badges: Wear your ID badge visibly at all times when in the office or at a client site. Remind others to do the same politely.
  • Tailgating: Do not let strangers follow you through a secure door without swiping their own badge. This is called "tailgating" and is a common way intruders get inside.
  • Clear desk policy: When you leave for the day, lock away sensitive documents. Do not leave client files, printed contracts, or personal data visible on your desk overnight.
  • Visitors: All visitors must sign in at reception and be escorted by a staff member. Do not let visitors wander around the office unattended.
  • Report any lost or stolen access badges to reception and IT immediately.

Keeping and deleting records

  • Some records must be kept for a minimum time required by law:
  • Financial records: 7 years minimum.
  • Employee records: Check with HR for current requirements.
  • Client contracts: Keep for the life of the contract plus 7 years.
  • When records no longer need to be kept, delete them securely. Do not just move them to the recycling bin — use approved secure deletion tools (ask IT).
  • Legal hold: If we are involved in a legal dispute or investigation, we may place a "legal hold" on certain records. This means you must not delete or alter them, even if they would normally be due for deletion. Legal or IT will notify you if this applies to you.
If you work at a client site, you must also follow the client's on-site security rules in addition to ours. If there is a conflict between their rules and ours, follow the stricter of the two and tell your manager.
